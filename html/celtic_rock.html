Celtic Rock music

Celtic music is usually thought of as irish or scottish because of the destinctive style of music they've both produce. many celtic musicians like paddy moloney have said that all the generes of celtic music are actully quite similar. in celtic music it is very normal for the melodic line to move up and down the primary chords.

when talking about celtic music you must know that there are many celtic regions. These include; Scotland, ireland, brittany and wales. These places keep hold of the traditions such as the language (gaelic) and the music.

in the atlantic canada their traditional music is heavly influenced by the irish and scottish in places such as Newfoundland in canada celtic music is as popular if not even more popular then it used to be.

Celtic instruments

Fiddle

A fiddle is a bow stringed instrument, it is like a violin but with a difference the difference between the two is the bridge. A fiddles bridge is flatter then that of a violin. This is because in folk music (such as the celtic music) the fiddle players will need use of more then one string at once.

Banjo

A banjo is a four five or six stringed it is a member of the guitar family with a round open-backed soundbox or parchment over a metal hoop. Traditional irish players usually use the four stringed banjo as a melody instrument.

Flute

The flute is a wind instrument which is held vertically or horizontally so the breath of the player strikes a narrow edge. flutes were not made for folk music but were adapted to it instead it was used for dance music.
